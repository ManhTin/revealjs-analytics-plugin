La Mer

Claude Debussy's Orchestral Masterpiece

A Musical Journey Through the Sea

Overview

  • Composed between 1903-1905
  • Premiered on October 15, 1905 in Paris
  • Called "Three symphonic sketches" by Debussy avoiding the term symphony
  • second of Debussy's three orchestral works

Composition

  • Written mostly away from the sea mostly inspired by artworks
  • Completed in Burgundy in 1905
  • Innovative orchestration techniques
  • Challenged traditional symphonic forms

Initial Reception

Premiered by the Orchestre Lamoureux in Paris on October 15, 1905 in Paris
Initially mixed reviews by critics and other musicians

"I do not hear, I do not see, I do not smell the sea"

Pierre Lalo - Le Temps

"The audience seemed rather disappointed: they expected the ocean, something big, something colossal, but they were served instead with some agitated water in a saucer"

Louis Schneider

Well-reviewed by musicians including Paul Dukas, Alfred Bruneau and Pierre de Bréville
Later recognized as a masterpiece of impressionism in music

Musical Legacy

  • Influenced modern orchestral composition
  • British composers Frank Merrick and Hope Squire arranged La Mer for piano duet and performed it in 1915
  • Pioneered impressionistic orchestration
  • Remains a cornerstone of the orchestral repertoire
